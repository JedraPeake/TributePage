David Livingstone

"If you have men who will only come if they know there is a good road, I don't want them. I want men who will come if there is no road at all."

Here is a timeline of his life:

  • 1813-Born on the 19th March in Blantyre, South Lanarkshire, Scotland
  • 1840-Completed his medical studies at various institutions in London, England
  • 1841-Moved to Africa, arriving in Cape Town, South Africa
  • 1849-He came upon Lake Ngami
  • 1851-He found the Zambezi River
  • 1853-He reached the western coastal region of Luanda
  • 1855-He came across the Zambezi falls, called by native populations "Smoke That Thunders" and which Livingstone dubbed Victoria Falls, after Queen Victoria
  • 1856-He had gone across the continent from west to east, arriving at the coastal region of Quelimane in what is present-day Mozambique
  • 1857-published Missionary Travels and Researches in South Africa
  • 1862-His wife, Mary, died from fever upon returning to Africa
  • 1864-Livingstone returned to England again in speaking out against slavery
  • 1865-He published Narrative of an Expedition to the Zambesi and Its Tributaries
  • 1871-With Livingstone thought to be lost, a transatlantic venture was sent to Africa to find Livingstone. Stanley located the physician in Ujiji in late 1871, and upon seeing him, uttered the now-well-known words, "Dr. Livingstone, I presume?"
  • 1872-He and Stanley parted ways
  • 1873-He died on May from malaria at the age of 60
